Docker Security 1

Hi.

This is the first article of a series of articles about Docker security.

Do you run containers? I guess you do.

And if I ask you how secure your container's deployment is, what would you answer me?

Do you scan your images before running them on production?

Do you run your dockerized application as "root"?

How do you pass sensitive data, like certs and passwords, to your containers?

Are you 100% sure that the image you pulled from your registry is actually the image you pushed earlier?

Well, these questions and others are what this series of articles is all about.

I'm not a security expert, but I started to think about all these questions and decided to take a better look into them.

Of course, there are many more security-related issues than what I'm going to expose here, but I think these articles would be a good starting point.

Following are some of the topics I'm intending to address here:

  • Is the application code running inside your container secure?.
  • How is your container image configured?
  • How secure is the machine where you build your container images?
  • How do you reliably pull and push images?
  • How should one run or configure containers?
  • How secure is the machine where you run your containers?
  • How do you pass secrets to your containers?
  • How do your containers securely use the network?
  • How to deal with container escape vulnerabilities?

As I'm writing these articles I'm also learning with you. So, I'd like to apologize in advance for any inconsistencies you may find and I really hope you like it.

As always, stay tuned!
